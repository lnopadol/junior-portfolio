The Idea Behind This Portfolio

This is a long-term, globally diversified portfolio built for a THB-based investor. The goal is simple: grow wealth steadily over 20+ years while staying protected against inflation, currency swings, and market crashes. Instead of betting on one country or one asset type, it spreads your money across four distinct roles — each serving a clear purpose.

Fixed Income (30%)
The anchor. Thai and Asian bonds provide steady income with low volatility. This is the part that keeps the portfolio stable when stocks drop.
Equities (55%)
The growth engine. Stocks from the US, Europe, Japan, Asia-Pacific, and emerging markets give you exposure to the world's best companies and fastest-growing economies.
Alternatives (10%)
The inflation shield. Commodities like gold and infrastructure assets tend to rise when prices go up — protecting your purchasing power over time.
Hedges (5%)
The safety net. A trend-following strategy that can profit during market crashes when everything else is falling. Think of it as portfolio insurance.

The portfolio is also positioned for a world where the US dollar gradually weakens — about 60% of holdings benefit from or are neutral to dollar depreciation.

If one sector dominates (say, 40% in Tech), your returns depend heavily on that single industry. Diversification Analysis

Correlation, risk metrics, and dollar weakening impact

Diversification means not putting all your eggs in one basket. This page measures how well your holdings complement each other. The correlation matrix shows whether two funds tend to move in the same direction (bad for diversification) or in opposite directions (good). A score of +1 means they move identically; −1 means they move in opposite directions; 0 means they're unrelated. The diversification score rates how spread out your portfolio is across holdings, countries, sectors, and sleeves. The dollar impact table shows how each holding is affected if the US dollar weakens over time — an important factor for Thai investors.

Macro Projection Notes

Slow Dollar Erosion Scenario

With ~60% of holdings in non-USD assets, a gradual USD decline of 2–3% annually could add 1–2% to portfolio returns via currency translation. Commodities (HGER) and EM (EMXC) benefit most. DBMF's trend-following adapts dynamically.

Current Political Climate

Rising geopolitical fragmentation, de-dollarization trends, and fiscal expansion in G7 support the inflation-hedge positioning (HGER, IGF). Thailand's fixed income provides stability. Japan (DXJ) benefits from yen weakness cycle. EM ex-China (EMXC) captures supply chain diversification flows.
